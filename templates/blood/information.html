Eligibility for blood donation

Age: Typically, donors must be at least 17 years old, although the minimum age requirement may vary. Some countries and organizations may allow 16-year-olds to donate with parental consent.

Weight: Donors usually need to meet a minimum weight requirement, often around 110 pounds (50 kg).

Health: Donors must be in good general health. You should not have any acute or chronic medical conditions that might be a concern for the recipient or the donor's safety. Common health issues like colds, flu, or minor infections may temporarily disqualify you from donating.

Medications and Medical History: Some medical conditions, medications, or treatments may disqualify potential donors. For example, individuals taking specific medications, having certain medical conditions, or having a history of specific diseases may not be eligible.

Tattoos and Piercings: Depending on local regulations, recent tattoos or piercings may result in deferral. The waiting period can vary.
